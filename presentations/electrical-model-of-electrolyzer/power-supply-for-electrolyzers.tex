          electrolyzers \vspace{0.5Em}
          \end{itemize}
      \item Targets for 2050 \vspace{0.5Em}
          \begin{itemize}
          \item Increase renewable electrolyzer capacity to at least 500 GW
          \end{itemize}  
      \end{itemize}
    \end{frame}

\section{Basic Principle of Water Electrolysis}
  \begin{frame}{Basic Principle of Water Electrolysis}
    \begin{columns}[t]
      \begin{column}{.4\textwidth}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Process of passing electric current through a substance 
            to induce a chemical change (oxidation and reduction) \vspace{0.5Em}
            \item Electrolyzer is a device which uses electrolysis to split water molecules 
            into hydrogen and oxygen \footnote{Water needs to be pre-processed; \\ 
            1 kg \ce{H_2} production requires $\approx$ 9 L processed water}  
            \\ \vspace{1.5Em}
            \centerline{\ce{H_2O} (l) = \ce{H_2} (g) + \ce{1/2O_2} (g)}
        \end{itemize}
      \end{column}

\section{Power Requirements for Electrolyzers}
  \begin{frame}{System Characteristics}
    \begin{columns}[t]
      \begin{column}{.5\textwidth}
        \begin{itemize}
          \item \textcolor{red}{Low voltage} and \textcolor{red}{high current} device \vspace{0.2Em}
          \item Ideal hydrogen production rate \\ \vspace{0.2Em}
          \begin{center}
            $\dot{n}_{\ce{H_2}} = \eta\dfrac{i_{\text{E}}}{n_{\text{e}}F}$ \vspace{0.75Em}
          \end{center}
          \item Nonlinear and dynamic behavior
        \end{itemize}
      \end{column}
      \begin{column}{.5\textwidth}
      \end{column}
    \end{columns} \vspace{0.2Em}

    \begin{flushleft}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Partial-load operation limits; \\ 20\% for AEL and 5\% for PEMEL 
        of nominal capacity \footfullcite{martinez_lopez_dynamic_2023} \vspace{0.2Em}
      \end{itemize}
    \end{flushleft}
      
  \end{frame}
  
  \begin{frame}{Supplied Power Quality}
    \begin{center}
      \def\svgwidth{0.6\textwidth}
      \input{electrolyzer-ratings.pdf_tex}
    \end{center}
    \begin{itemize}
        %\item Requires DC voltage and current as input \vspace{0.2Em}
        %\item Step-down and high current capabilities \vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Harmonic frequency in range $>\text{300 Hz}$ and $<\text{100 Hz}$ have negligible effect 
        on efficiency \footfullcite{ruuskanen_power_2020} \vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Further study ongoing to estimate the effect of current harmonics on 
        electrolyzer lifespan and degradation \footfullcite{ruuskanen_power_2020}
    \end{itemize}    
  \end{frame}
  
  \section{State-of-the-Art Electrolyzer Power Supplies}
    \begin{frame}{VSC-based Power Supplies}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Back-to-back converter smoothens output current ripple\vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Lower specific energy consumption in comparison to 
        thyristor rectifiers \footfullcite{koponen_effect_of_converter_2019} \vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Higher failure rate of IGBTs at high current operation \vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Parallelizing required to meet high current demands,
        increased number of components increase cost and carbon footprint of the system \vspace{0.2Em}  
      \end{itemize} \vspace{1Em} 
      
      \begin{center}
        \def\svgwidth{0.9\textwidth}
        \input{vsc-based-rectifier.pdf_tex}
      \end{center}
    \end{frame}  

    \begin{frame}{LCC-based Power Supplies}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Low device losses \vspace{0.2Em}
        \item Can be directly connected to the electrolyzer, DC side inductance 
        can be ignored \footfullcite{iribarren_modelling_2022}
      \end{itemize}
      \begin{columns}
        \begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
          \begin{center}
            \def\svgwidth{0.9\textwidth}
            \input{six-pulse-thyristor-bridge.pdf_tex}
          \end{center}
        \end{column}
        \begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
          \begin{center}
            \def\svgwidth{0.9\textwidth}
            \input{12-pulse-individual-row.pdf_tex}
          \end{center}
        \end{column}
      \end{columns}  \vspace{1Em}  
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Generates significant reactive power, higher short-circuit power and 
        harmonic filters recommended to 
        comply with harmonic regulations \footfullcite{iribarren_asymmetrical_2023} \vspace{0.2Em}
        %\item Electrolyzer energy consumption 20\% higher than pure dc supply \vspace{0.2Em} %\footfullcite{koponen_effect_of_converter_2019} 
      \end{itemize}
    \end{frame}
